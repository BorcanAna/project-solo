Top 3 best Romanian footballers of all time

Football has always kept us close to each other, in the most beautiful moments of the Romanian teams: the Champions Cup final in 86' or the 1994 World Cup, in which the entire microbista breath enjoyed the greatest performance of the tricolors, and the list can go on. Who are the ones who transformed a simple sport into magical moments for the home team? Here is what the top of the best Romanian footballers of all time looks like:

  1. Ladislau Bölöni

    Twice elected "The best footballer of Romania", in 1977 and 1983, Bölöni played a fundamental role in the Steaua team in 1986. The footballer was also more than decisive in the configuration of the national team, for which he scored 102 selections and scored 22 goals. In the history of Romanian football, only four players have been called up to the national team more times than "Loti" Bölöni. He currently coaches the Royal Antwerp team in Belgium.

  2. Helmuth Duckadam

    “The Hero of Seville”, as the legendary goalkeeper is nicknamed, received this nickname after setting a world record in the 1986 European Cup final, won by Steaua Bucharest against Barcelona. Duckadam then saved four consecutive penalty kicks. At just 27 years old, his career was suddenly cut short by an aneurysm, which caused surgery on his right arm. For a period of three years, he was forced to miss sports activity, returning (for only two seasons) to the goal of Vagonul Arad.

  3. Dan Petrescu

    Without a doubt, he is the best right-back in the history of Romanian football. He began his professional career at Steaua Bucharest, for which he played from 1985 to 1991. Then followed his adventure, for more than a decade, through European football. First in Italy, at Foggia Calcio and Genoa, then moving on to British football. In England, Dan Petrescu played for Sheffield Wednesday, Chelsea, Bradford City and Southampton. For the national team, the defender scored 95 selections and scored 12 goals. He participated in four final tournaments: two world championships and two European championships.
